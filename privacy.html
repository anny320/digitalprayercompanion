🔐 Privacy First Model

Your prayer journal is sacred. It belongs only to you.

The Simple Version

Everything you write in your journal stays 100% on your device. We do not collect it. We cannot see it. No one can — not even your facilitator. Your reflections are between you and God.

How Your Journal Works

💾

Local Storage Only

Journal entries are saved to your browser's localStorage — a storage area on your device that never leaves it.

🚫

No Server Transmission

Nothing you type is sent to any server, database, or third party. The text stays in your browser.

🔑

You Own It

You can clear your journal at any time by clearing your browser data. You are always in control.

Community & Facilitators

No. Never. Your journal is stored locally on your device and is never transmitted anywhere. Your facilitator cannot access it — not through this website, not through any tool. Your reflections are completely private.
Anything you voluntarily share in the WhatsApp community groups is subject to normal WhatsApp privacy policies. We strongly encourage only sharing what you are comfortable with. Facilitators are instructed never to share anything you say privately or publicly in the group outside of it.
You can clear all local journal data at any time by going to your browser settings and clearing site data or localStorage for this site. On most browsers: Settings → Privacy → Clear browsing data → Local storage.
No account is required. The entire 21-day journey — including journaling, progress tracking, and all content — is accessible without signing up or providing any personal information.
Your journal entries exist in your browser's localStorage. If you clear your browser data or switch devices, they will not carry over. We recommend copying important reflections to a personal notes app or document if you want to preserve them long term.

The Privacy Principle

Your prayer journal is a sacred conversation between you and God. It is not data to be collected, analyzed, or shared. We built this with that conviction at the center. What you write is holy. It belongs to you.
